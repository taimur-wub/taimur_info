About

I am Taimur Rahman, an Assistant Professor in the Department of Civil Engineering at the World University of Bangladesh, Dhaka. My work sits at the intersection of structural engineering and machine learning, with a focus on turning complex analytical models and heterogeneous data into reliable tools for engineers.

My research spans fundamental period models for RC and steel braced RC buildings, masonry infilled frames, and probabilistic ML frameworks for UHPC and advanced concretes. I develop interpretable stacked ensembles, LightGBM and NGBoost models, and deep symbolic regression based expressions that complement structural mechanics, rather than replace it. The goal is simple: code compatible, transparent models that improve seismic assessment and design decisions.

In parallel, I teach core structural engineering courses, supervise undergraduate theses, serve on editorial and review boards, and lead initiatives that bring data driven thinking into everyday academic and professional practice. I am particularly interested in collaborations that link numerical simulation, experimental work, and ML for practical, deployable solutions in developing and seismic-prone regions.
